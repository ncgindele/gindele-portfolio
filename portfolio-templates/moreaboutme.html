(More) About Me

Early computing bio. I first got a taste of programming on the family i386 in the living room. My dad, clearly excited, asked me what I wanted to make, and naturally I said a recreation of the hacking program that John Conner used in one of the early scenes of Terminator 2 (I was too young to see the movie but I had caught segments, while my dad talked at length with the local audio/video store staff, a frequent occurance). I explained enough for my dad to get the general concept, and we made a program to guess a word one letter at a time. I remember the gratifying feeling of watching the characters stream down the monitor until one by one they fixed into place. I did my fair share of the coding, but every so often he'd tell me that he needed to do some "magic". It wasn't until high school that I really started to learn the ways of the magician. I spent two summers working full-time at the University of Rochester's Laboratory for Laser Energetics where I was nominally a physics intern. The day-to-day tasks were almost entirely programming: mostly writing data analytic utilities in PV-WAVE. I later took an AP course in C++ and received a medal and scholarship for computing from Rochester Institute of Technology.

Later bio. In college, philosophy most captured my passion, enough so that I decided to get my PhD. My dissertation was ultimately a philosophical treatment of the psychology, biology, and neuroscience of play in animals and humans. While in graduate school, I built and maintained my own PC and audited a Java course Data Structures and Algorithms; however, it wasn't until leaving graduate school that I realized I should seriously pursue coding as a career. Since then I've studied Java and later Python with a focus on web development. In that time, I've completed the Udacity Full-stack Nanodegree and done a number of political science oriented consulting projects using Python to process data and web scrape. I currently develop web apps and provide data solutions on a freelance basis.  Back
